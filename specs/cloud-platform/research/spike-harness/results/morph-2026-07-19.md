## Broke on fork (and workaround)

- **Nothing in the workload broke** — identical to the E2B result.
- **Identity does NOT re-key** (same as E2B): `boot_id` and `/etc/machine-id`
  inherited by all descendants; re-key at the Zuse layer.
- **Source pauses during branch (~4.3 s)** — unlike E2B's zero-interruption
  snapshot. Fine for our lifecycle (fork at natural pauses), but visible if
  forking under a live user session.
- `exposeHttpService` is not idempotent (409 on re-expose); treat as
  upsert-with-catch in the provisioner.

Same as the E2B sheet: saturation, multi-hour pause/billing audit, failure

## Verdict

**All gates pass on Morph too — the fork primitive is real on both shortlisted
providers.** Differences that matter: E2B snapshots without pausing the source
(0 ms vs ~4.3 s) and forks in ~1.7 s vs 11.8 s for the atomic branch call;
Morph is ~40% cheaper at list price, boots instances faster (0.73 s), and
gives full-VM/root semantics. The v1 decision (start on E2B) stands; Morph is
now a **proven** cheaper alternate rather than a paper one, de-risking the
scale/pricing card.
