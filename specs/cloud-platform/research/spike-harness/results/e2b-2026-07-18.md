## Broke on fork (and workaround)

- **Nothing in the workload broke.** Sockets, PTY-less background processes,
  open SQLite handles, recursive watchers, Xvfb, Electron, and Chromium all
  survived.
- **Identity does NOT re-key itself:** `/proc/.../boot_id` and
  `/etc/machine-id` are identical across source and all descendants. Fork
  identity must be assigned at the app layer (input to the fork-identity
  decision ticket).
- Tunnel: `cloudflared` process survived; which descendant the pre-fork
  trycloudflare URL routes to was not isolated in this pass — needs a focused
  follow-up alongside the fork-identity decision.
- Clocks: wall clock is correct post-fork; monotonic clock continuity was
  captured in `e2b-state.json` for the identity ticket to inspect.

## Cost observed

Entire leg (1 source + 10 descendants + template build, ~25 machine-minutes):
well under $1 at listed rates ($0.000014/vCPU-s + $0.0000045/GiB-s).
Snapshot storage: not billed during the run window; retention pricing still
unmeasured. Free-tier constraints hit: 1 h max sandbox lifetime (fine for
per-attempt runs; irrelevant at Pro's 24 h).

## Verdict

**All 13 gates pass; no fallback needed.** E2B empirically supports the full
target machine — live-process one-to-many fork included — at latencies an
order of magnitude inside the roadmap's thresholds. The two doc-level fears
(local Chromium/CDP unproven, connections dropped during snapshot) did not
materialize: CDP worked and not a single 250 ms health probe failed during the
snapshot. Provider lock still awaits the Morph result sheet (Morph leg
currently blocked on account billing).
